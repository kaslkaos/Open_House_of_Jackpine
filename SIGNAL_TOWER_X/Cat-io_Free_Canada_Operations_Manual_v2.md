## THE TECHNIQUE

**Cat-io Free Canada is Aesopian language.**

Named after Aesop's fables — animal stories that carry political meaning. The term was formalized to describe how Russian and Soviet dissidents communicated under censorship: say the true thing through the sidewise thing. The animals carry what cannot be said directly. The censor can't quite object because *it's just a story about cats and penguins.* But everyone who needs to understand, understands.

**How it works in practice:**

The Purr Minister's dispatches run parallel to real events. Variable geometry purrtnerships map to actual diplomatic agreements. The Penguin Empire's origin tracks real tariff absurdity. The Geese deployments mirror actual defence postures. The cat knocks FAFO off the table because FAFO was posted by the White House.

**The key:** Never break the frame to explain. The fable IS the explanation. If someone doesn't get it, the cat doesn't care. If someone does get it, the message lands harder than a direct statement ever could.

**Why it works:**
- Humor bypasses defensive reading
- Absurdity matches the absurdity it describes
- Sharing is easy (people share cats)
- Plausible deniability is itself cat-like
- The serious point lands *harder* through the sidewise approach

**The tradition:**
- Aesop (animal fables carrying political truths)
- Krylov (Russian fabulist under the Tsars)
- Samizdat humor (Soviet-era underground wit)
- Orwell (Animal Farm — the genre's masterwork)
- Cat parody diplomacy (now, here, us)
