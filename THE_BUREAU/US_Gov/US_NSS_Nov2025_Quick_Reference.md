# US National Security Strategy (November 2025) - Quick Reference

**Date:** November 2025  
**Administration:** Trump (second term)  
**Pages:** 33

## Key Principles (p. 8-11)

1. **Focused Definition of National Interest** - narrowing scope
2. **Peace Through Strength** - deterrence through dominance
3. **Predisposition to Non-Interventionism** - high bar for intervention
4. **Flexible Realism** - not imposing democratic change
5. **Primacy of Nations** - nations putting own interests first
6. **Sovereignty and Respect** - protecting US sovereignty from international bodies
7. **Balance of Power** - preventing regional domination by others
8. **Pro-American Worker** - prioritizing US workers over growth
9. **Fairness** - ending "free-riding" by allies
10. **Competence and Merit** - opposition to DEI, but "America First" in hiring

---

## Key Priorities (p. 11-15)

### The Era of Mass Migration Is Over (p. 11)
- Border security = primary element of national security
- Mass migration described as straining resources, increasing crime, weakening cohesion
- "Border controlled by will of American people...fundamental to survival"

### Protection of Core Rights and Liberties (p. 12)
- Opposition to "deradicalization" and "protecting our democracy" as pretexts
- Advocacy for these principles among democratic allies
- Opposition to "elite-driven, anti-democratic restrictions" in Europe/Anglosphere

### Burden-Sharing and Burden-Shifting (p. 12)
- **Hague Commitment:** NATO countries to spend 5% of GDP on defense
- US as "convener and supporter" rather than primary actor
- Allies to assume "primary responsibility for their regions"

### Realignment Through Peace (p. 13)
- Peace deals to increase stability and influence
- Opens new markets through diplomatic realignment

### Economic Security (p. 13-15)
- **Balanced Trade** - reducing deficits, opposing barriers
- **Critical Supply Chains** - never dependent on outside powers (Hamilton reference)
- **Reindustrialization** - "re-shoring" production, strategic tariffs
- **Defense Industrial Base** - national mobilization, scale production
- **Energy Dominance** - oil/gas/coal/nuclear, rejecting "climate change ideology"
- **Financial Sector Dominance** - preserving USD reserve status, digital finance leadership

## Western Hemisphere: The Trump Corollary to the Monroe Doctrine (p. 15-19)

### Core Framework
**"Enlist and Expand"** approach

### Stated Goals
- Control migration
- Stop drug flows
- Deny "non-Hemispheric competitors" ability to position forces or own strategic assets
- "Make America preeminent in Western Hemisphere as condition of security and prosperity"

### Specific Actions Outlined
- **Military presence readjustment:** shifting resources to hemisphere
- **Coast Guard/Navy presence:** control sea lanes, stop migration, trafficking
- **Targeted deployments:** secure border, "defeat cartels, including where necessary use of lethal force"
- **Strategic locations:** establishing/expanding access

### Economic Approach
- Commercial diplomacy prioritized
- Tariffs and reciprocal trade as "powerful tools"
- Goal: partners build domestic economies while becoming US market
- Critical supply chains in hemisphere reduce dependencies

### Countering "Outside Influence"
- Major focus on rolling back foreign (implicitly Chinese) infrastructure control
- "Winding down adversarial outside influence" as condition of aid/alliances
- US to be "partner of first choice" through:
  - Demonstrating hidden costs of foreign assistance (espionage, debt-traps)
  - Expediting US approvals/licensing
  - Leveraging finance/technology access

### Private Sector Role
- US embassies to track business opportunities
- All officials to help US companies compete and succeed
- Government financing programs to support US goods purchases
- "Sole-source contracts" for countries most dependent on US
- "Push out foreign companies that build infrastructure in region"

### CRITICAL FOR CANADIAN SOVEREIGNTY:
**Direct language on strategic acquisitions and forcing out competitors**
**Explicit framing of hemisphere as US sphere requiring "preeminence"**
**"Terms of alliances...contingent on winding down adversarial outside influence"**

## Asia: Win the Economic Future (p. 19-25)

### Economic Focus
- Rebalancing China relationship (reciprocity/fairness)
- Goal: US $30T → $40T economy in 2030s
- **Combined allied economic power:** US $30T + allies $35T = $65T (>half world economy)

### What Must End (p. 21)
- Predatory subsidies and industrial strategies
- Unfair trading practices
- "Job destruction and deindustrialization"
- IP theft and industrial espionage
- Supply chain threats (minerals, rare earths)
- Fentanyl precursor exports
- "Propaganda, influence operations, cultural subversion"

### Technology Priorities
- Preserve advantage in: undersea, space, nuclear
- Future domains: AI, quantum computing, autonomous systems
- Energy to fuel these domains

### Critical Infrastructure
- Government-private sector relationships for network surveillance
- "Real-time discovery, attribution, and response"
- Network defense and offensive cyber operations

### Diplomatic/Economic Strategy
- Pressing allies to adopt trade policies helping "rebalance China's economy toward household consumption"
- Joint plan for "Global South" using:
  - Europe/Japan/South Korea net foreign assets: $7T
  - Multilateral development banks: $1.5T combined assets
- America as "global partner of first choice" through tech dominance
- **Coalitions using "comparative advantages in finance and technology"**
- **Binding currencies to dollar to ensure reserve currency status**

### Taiwan & First Island Chain (p. 23-24)
- Deterring conflict over Taiwan = priority
- Semiconductor production dominance noted
- Taiwan provides "direct access to Second Island Chain"
- One-third global shipping through South China Sea
- **Goal:** military capable of "denying aggression anywhere in First Island Chain"
- Allies must provide greater access to ports/facilities
- Focus on capabilities to deter Taiwan seizure

## Europe: Promoting European Greatness (p. 25-27)

### Diagnosis
- Economic decline: 25% global GDP (1990) → 14% (today)
- **Real problem identified as "civilizational erasure"**

### Issues Listed
- EU/transnational bodies undermining "political liberty and sovereignty"
- Migration policies "transforming continent and creating strife"
- "Censorship of free speech and suppression of political opposition"
- Cratering birthrates
- "Loss of national identities and self-confidence"

### Direct Quote (p. 25):
**"Should present trends continue, the continent will be unrecognizable in 20 years or less."**

### Russia/Ukraine
- European hard power advantage over Russia "by almost every measure, save nuclear weapons"
- US interest: "expeditious cessation of hostilities in Ukraine"
- Goals: stabilize European economies, prevent escalation, reestablish strategic stability with Russia

### Democracy Critique
- "European officials who hold unrealistic expectations for war perched in unstable minority governments"
- **"Many of which trample on basic principles of democracy to suppress opposition"**
- "Large European majority wants peace, yet not translated into policy...because of governments' subversion of democratic processes"

### US Policy
- "Stand up for genuine democracy, freedom of expression"
- Encourage "unapologetic celebrations of European nations' individual character and history"
- Support "political allies in Europe to promote this revival of spirit"
- "Growing influence of patriotic European parties indeed gives cause for great optimism"

### NATO Future Concerns
- **"Within a few decades at the latest, certain NATO members will become majority non-European"**
- Open question whether they'll view alliance with US "in same way as those who signed NATO charter"

### Priorities for Europe (p. 27)
- Reestablishing stability within Europe and with Russia
- Europe operating as "aligned sovereign nations" taking primary defense responsibility
- "Cultivating resistance to Europe's current trajectory"
- Opening markets to US goods
- Building up Central/Eastern/Southern Europe
- **"Ending perception, and preventing reality, of NATO as perpetually expanding alliance"**
- Encouraging action against "mercantilist overcapacity, technological theft, cyber espionage"

## Middle East & Africa (p. 27-29)

### Middle East: Shift Burdens, Build Peace
- Region no longer dominating US foreign policy
- Focus on partnership, friendship, investment
- **Abraham Accords expansion** as goal
- Transition from security focus to economic partnership

### Africa (p. 29)
- Transition from "foreign aid paradigm to investment and growth paradigm"
- Focus on "abundant natural resources and latent economic potential"
- Energy sector and critical mineral development as immediate investment areas
- Nuclear energy, LPG, LNG technologies for "competition for critical minerals"

## Language & Framing Notes

### On International Institutions
- "Transnationalism that explicitly seeks to dissolve individual state sovereignty" (p. 2)
- "Sovereignty-sapping incursions of most intrusive transnational organizations" (p. 9)
- International institutions "driven by outright anti-Americanism" (p. 2)

### On Globalization
- "Hugely misguided and destructive bets on globalism and so-called 'free trade'" (p. 1)
- "Hollowed out very middle class and industrial base" (p. 1)

### On Democracy/Rights
- Opposes "deradicalization" and "protecting our democracy" as pretexts for power abuse (p. 12)
- Critiques European governments for "subversion of democratic processes" (p. 26)
- Supports "patriotic European parties" and "resistance to Europe's current trajectory" (p. 26-27)

### On Climate
- **"We reject the disastrous 'climate change' and 'Net Zero' ideologies"** (p. 14)

### On DEI
- **"Rooting out so-called 'DEI' and other discriminatory and anti-competitive practices"** (p. 6)
- "Radical ideologies that seek to replace competence and merit with favored group status" (p. 11)

## Canadian Sovereignty Implications

### Direct Threats Referenced
1. **Hemispheric "Preeminence" Doctrine** - explicit framing of Western Hemisphere as US sphere
2. **Strategic Asset Control** - "deny competitors ability to own or control strategically vital assets"
3. **Alliance Contingency** - aid/alliances contingent on "winding down adversarial outside influence"
4. **Economic Coercion Tools** - tariffs, market access, technology leverage as strategic tools
5. **"Sole-Source Contracts"** - for countries "most dependent on US"

### Systemic Concerns
- Rejection of international institutions and multilateralism
- Explicit opposition to "transnationalism" and "sovereignty-sapping" international bodies
- Currency dominance strategy ("bind currencies more closely to dollar")
- Technology control ("ensure U.S. technology and U.S. standards...drive world forward")
- Infrastructure/supply chain control in hemisphere

### Democracy/Rights Concerns
- Opposition to what document frames as "elite-driven, anti-democratic restrictions"
- Support for "patriotic parties" resisting current governance in allied democracies
- Critique of governments "suppressing opposition" under democracy rhetoric
- Framing of own immigration enforcement as "fundamental to survival"

## Notable Absences

- **Climate change** rejected as "disastrous ideology"
- **Human rights** as independent concern (only "core rights" for US citizens)
- **International law** (focus on sovereignty over legal frameworks)
- **Multilateral cooperation** (replaced with "burden-shifting" and bilateral deals)
- **Democratic governance** as goal for other nations (replaced with "flexible realism")

---

## Document Tone & Style

- Confident, assertive, declarative
- Explicitly rejects previous administrations' approaches
- Frames Trump policies as "necessary correction"
- Uses blunt language ("invasion," "civilizational erasure," "forever wars")
- Presents US interests as requiring dominance, not partnership
- Treats sovereignty as zero-sum (more for others = less for US)
