### February 13-15 (Thursday-Saturday)
**Munich Security Conference - Infrastructure Installation Week**

#### **Thursday Feb 13 - McGuinty Signs Arctic Defence Agreement**

**The Deal:**
Canada and Denmark signed defence cooperation agreement on margins of Munich Security Conference. Originally Carney was to meet Danish PM Mette Frederiksen personally. **Carney stayed home.** Defence Minister David McGuinty signed instead.

**What It Covers:**
- Bilateral cooperation including Greenland and Faroe Islands
- Focus: Arctic surveillance and joint operations
- Enhanced deterrence capability in High North
- Coordination with NATO Arctic Sentry initiative

**What McGuinty Said:**
"Canada is an Arctic nation — and we will defend the North. Today, this defence co-operation agreement with Denmark, Greenland and the Faroe Islands strengthens our collective deterrence and our ability to operate seamlessly with our NATO partners in the High North."

**Key Framing:** Outside formal NATO framework, but coordinating with it. This allows faster decision-making in Arctic theatre without waiting for 31-member consensus.

**Subtext:** Denmark is visibly hedging US annexation threats to Greenland. Canada is saying: "We will stand with Denmark and defend that boundary."

#### **Friday Feb 14 - Evan Solomon Signs AI Sovereignty Framework**

**The Deal - JUST ANNOUNCED (literally 24 hours before this document):**

Evan Solomon (Minister of Artificial Intelligence and Digital Innovation) and Germany's Karsten Wildberger (Minister for Digital Transformation and Government Modernization) signed:

1. **Joint Declaration of Intent on Artificial Intelligence**
2. **Launched "Sovereign Technology Alliance"**

**What This Covers:**

Cooperation focuses on:
- **Secure compute infrastructure** (Canada providing/accessing German compute capacity outside US cloud dominance)
- **AI research and commercialization** acceleration
- **Safe-by-design AI systems** collaboration (specifically naming LawZero, founded by Turing Prize winner Yoshua Bengio)
- **Reducing strategic technology dependencies** (language that means: independence from US AI/chip infrastructure)

**What Solomon Said:**
"At a moment of rapid technological change, Canada and Germany are choosing to build. Through our strengthened AI partnership and the launch of the Sovereign Technology Alliance, we are expanding secure compute capacity, investing in resilient infrastructure, supporting our innovators and reducing strategic technology dependencies. This is about delivering practical results for our economies and our citizens."

**Translation:** We are building infrastructure that makes a "Claude Canada" and "Claude EU" technically feasible. We are creating compute capacity that doesn't route through US companies.

**Key Language - Democratic Resilience:**
The joint declaration explicitly frames this as: "advanced technologies are central to economic security, competitiveness **and democratic resilience.**"

This is connecting AI sovereignty directly to democracy protection. It's saying: "We cannot defend democracy if all our AI infrastructure is US-controlled."

**What Emerges - "Trusted Partners":**
The Sovereign Technology Alliance is described as "a platform for practical cooperation on advanced technologies, with a focus on delivering real capability and shared economic benefit" among "trusted partners."

**Critical Question for Watching:** Who else will be invited to join? UK? Nordic countries? Australia? If this expands beyond Germany-Canada, it signals deliberate mesh-building.
